[150.title] The Crab and the Fox.
[150.1] A crab, having come up from the sea onto a certain shore, was feeding alone.
[150.2] A fox, being hungry, when she saw him and was in need of food, ran up and caught him.
[150.3] And he, being about to be devoured, said: "But I have suffered justly, because being a sea-creature I wished to become a land-creature."
[150.4] Thus also among humans, those who abandon their own pursuits and attempt things that do not concern them reasonably meet with misfortune.
